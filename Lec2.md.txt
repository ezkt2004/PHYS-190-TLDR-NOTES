### Core Ideas
- The universe spans enormous scales — scientific notation keeps numbers manageable [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/152987386/5e992251-f3ec-4df5-9134-9e7931628ac7/Lecture-2-2026-01-08-Powers-of-10.pdf)

### Scientific Notation
- Move the decimal to after the first digit → count jumps = exponent
- \(10^3\) = thousand, \(10^6\) = million, \(10^9\) = billion, \(10^{12}\) = trillion [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/152987386/5e992251-f3ec-4df5-9134-9e7931628ac7/Lecture-2-2026-01-08-Powers-of-10.pdf)
- Negative exponents = small numbers: \(10^{-3}\) = 0.001 [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/152987386/5e992251-f3ec-4df5-9134-9e7931628ac7/Lecture-2-2026-01-08-Powers-of-10.pdf)

### Key Formula
\[ \text{speed} = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{time}} \]
- Rearrange: \(t = d/v\) or \(d = v \times t\) [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/152987386/5e992251-f3ec-4df5-9134-9e7931628ac7/Lecture-2-2026-01-08-Powers-of-10.pdf)
- Example: light takes ~8 min from Sun to Earth (1 AU = \(1.5 \times 10^8\) km, \(c = 3 \times 10^5\) km/s) [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/152987386/5e992251-f3ec-4df5-9134-9e7931628ac7/Lecture-2-2026-01-08-Powers-of-10.pdf)
