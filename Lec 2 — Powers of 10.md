# 📚 Lec 2 — Powers of 10 & Scale of Universe

## Core Ideas
- Universe spans enormous scales → scientific notation keeps numbers manageable
- Astronomers round to nearest **factor of 10** (order-of-magnitude estimation)

## Scientific Notation
- Move decimal to after first digit → count jumps = exponent
- 10³ = thousand | 10⁶ = million | 10⁹ = billion | 10¹² = trillion
- Negative exponents = small numbers: 10⁻³ = 0.001

## Key Formula
speed = distance / time
→ t = d / v
→ d = v × t

**Example:** Light from Sun to Earth
- 1 AU = 1.5 × 10⁸ km
- c = 3 × 10⁵ km/s
- t = 1.5×10⁸ / 3×10⁵ = 500 s ≈ 8 min

## Units to Know
| Unit | Value |
|------|-------|
| 1 AU | 1.5 × 10⁸ km |
| 1 light-year | ~10¹⁶ m |
| Speed of light | 3 × 10⁵ km/s |

## Angles
- Full circle = 360°
- 1° = 60 arcminutes (')
- 1' = 60 arcseconds ('')
- Moon's angular diameter ≈ 0.5°
